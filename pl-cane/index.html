I never thought I’d see my Mom on the ground. Not like that.

But one morning, I got a call I hope you never receive. My Dad was upset and barely able to make sense of what he was saying.

I rushed over, only to find my Mom still writhing in pain on the ground. Unable to pick herself back up.

My Dad had found her lying in the hallway. She had tried to get up in the night to use the bathroom, and her cane—the one we all assumed was "fine"—had folded underneath her.

Her hip had a bone bruise. Her wrist was sprained. And worst of all, her confidence was shattered.

She refused to get out of bed the next day. She stopped wanting to go for walks. That one fall didn’t just shake her body—it shook her independence.

Because this fall could have been fatal.
